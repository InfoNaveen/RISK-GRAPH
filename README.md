## What is RiskGraph?

Most portfolio tools treat risk as a number. RiskGraph treats it as a **network**.

Assets are nodes. Correlations are edges. Systemic risk propagates through the graph the same way contagion moves through a population — not uniformly, but along the paths of highest connectivity. The asset at the center of the network doesn't just carry its own risk; it amplifies everyone else's.

RiskGraph 2.0 makes this visible, interactive, and mathematically rigorous. Every metric on screen has a derivation. Every chart has a formula behind it. Every slider changes real math in real time.

Built on Next.js 14, deployed on Vercel, no external data dependencies. All simulation is synthetic, deterministic, and reproducible.

## Modules

### `01` Network Graph
**Route:** `/network`

The core of RiskGraph. A live d3-force simulation of 10 Indian equity assets modeled as a weighted undirected graph G = (V, E, w).

- Node radius scales with **eigenvector centrality** — the most systemically connected assets appear largest
- Edge weight encodes **Pearson correlation** — gold edges signal correlation > 0.6
- Click any node to highlight its 1-hop neighborhood and dim the periphery
- Real-time computation of **GSVI**, **Spectral Risk Ratio**, and **Network Density**
- Sortable centrality table: Eigenvector · Betweenness · Weighted Degree · Risk Tier

The **GSVI** (Graph-Weighted Systemic Vulnerability Index) is the headline metric:

```
GSVI = Σᵢ wᵢ · cᵢ     where cᵢ = 0.6 · c_eig(i) + 0.4 · c_bet(i)
```

A portfolio concentrated in high-centrality assets has maximum exposure to correlated shocks. GSVI makes that exposure a single number.

### `02` Monte Carlo Simulation
**Route:** `/monte-carlo`

10,000-path Geometric Brownian Motion simulation with full cross-asset correlation via Cholesky decomposition.

```
S(t+1) = S(t) · exp[(μ - σ²/2)Δt + σ√Δt · Z]     Z ~ N(0,1)
```

The Itô correction term `−σ²/2` is not optional. It ensures `E[S(t)] = S(0)·e^{μt}` — without it, the simulation overstates expected returns.

- Correlated returns sampled via: `R = μ + LZ` where `Σ = LLᵀ` (Cholesky)
- Normal variates generated by Box-Muller transform — no external random library
- Chart renders 200 sampled paths + 5th / 50th / 95th percentile overlays
- Live computation of **VaR(95%)**, **CVaR(95%)**, **Sharpe Ratio**, **Probability of Loss**
- Portfolio value formatted in Indian number system throughout (₹X,XX,XXX)

### `03` Stress Testing & Contagion
**Route:** `/stress`

Two distinct stress models in one module.

**PSD-Safe Stress Operator** — slides the covariance matrix from realized toward worst-case:

```
Σ_stress(α) = (1−α)Σ + α·Σ_max     where Σ_max = σσᵀ
```

Σ_max is the outer product of individual volatilities — the matrix where all correlations equal 1. The convex combination preserves positive semi-definiteness at every α ∈ [0,1], guaranteeing valid Cholesky decomposition for downstream Monte Carlo.

**Capital Flow Contagion** — models herding behavior iteratively:

```
w(t+1) = normalize[(I + βA) · w(t)]
```

When β exceeds `1/λ_max(A)`, the spectral radius of the system matrix exceeds 1. The dominant eigenvector of A absorbs all portfolio weight — herding collapse. A live stability warning fires the moment you cross the threshold.

The **Diversification Ratio curve** tracks `DR = Σwᵢσᵢ / σ_p` across timesteps, showing exactly when the portfolio loses its diversification benefit as contagion propagates.

### `04` Net Worth & Tax
**Route:** `/networth`

Full Indian portfolio aggregation with FY2025-26 tax liability computation.

Inputs: equity holdings (10 assets), physical gold & silver, real estate, cash & FDs, liabilities (home loan, personal loan, credit card debt).

Tax engine implements the complete Budget 2024 + Budget 2025 rule set:
- **STCG on equity** (Section 111A): 20% flat
- **LTCG on equity** (Section 112A): 12.5%, ₹1,25,000 annual exemption
- **LTCG on gold/physical**: 12.5% flat (indexation removed Budget 2024)
- **New Regime slabs** (Budget 2025): NIL → 5% → 10% → 15% → 20% → 25% → 30%
- **Section 87A rebate**: full rebate if total income ≤ ₹12,00,000
- **Surcharge tiers**: 10% / 15% on income bands
- **Health & Education Cess**: 4% on (tax + surcharge)

### `07` Price History
**Route:** `/prices`


Same seed = same prices every load. Stable, reproducible, and realistic.

Starting prices approximate NSE FY2025-26 values: RELIANCE ₹2,850 · TCS ₹3,920 · HDFCBANK ₹1,680 · INFY ₹1,540 · ICICIBANK ₹1,220 · AXISBANK ₹1,080 · SBIN ₹780 · WIPRO ₹480 · LT ₹3,450 · MARUTI ₹12,200

## Mathematics

The application implements the following algorithms from scratch in TypeScript — no math libraries, no shortcuts:

**`lib/math.ts`**
- **Cholesky decomposition** `LLᵀ = Σ` with ε-ridge for numerical stability
- **Power iteration** for dominant eigenvector (50 iterations, tolerance 1e-6)
- **Brandes algorithm** for betweenness centrality — exact, O(VE)
- **Box-Muller transform** for standard normal sampling
- **GBM path simulation** with correlated Cholesky sampling
- **PSD-safe stress operator** `Σ_s(α) = (1−α)Σ + αΣ_max`
- **Contagion iteration** with spectral stability detection

**`lib/tax.ts`**
- New Regime slab computation with 87A rebate
- Old Regime slab computation with deduction handling
- STCG / LTCG classification and tax calculation
- Surcharge tier logic (capped at 15% for capital gains)
- Health & Education Cess on (tax + surcharge)

**`lib/priceData.ts`**
- Seeded LCG PRNG: `xₙ₊₁ = (a·xₙ + c) mod m`
- 252-day GBM history for 10 assets with realistic μ and σ
- Log-return computation for correlation and volatility statistics

## Ethical AI Usage

This project was developed for educational and research purposes under the following principles:

1. **Transparency** — All mathematical models, assumptions, and limitations are explicitly documented in `/math`. No black-box algorithms. Every metric has a derivation.

2. **Reproducibility** — All random processes use configurable seeds. Identical parameters produce identical results.

3. **No Investment Advice** — This is a research prototype. No output should be used for real trading decisions. The models presented are pedagogical, not advisory.

4. **Model Limitations** — The multivariate normal assumption underestimates tail risk. Real markets exhibit fat tails, skewness, regime switching, and liquidity effects not captured here. The Marchenko-Pastur bounds are computed assuming i.i.d. returns — a strong simplification.

5. **Data** — No real market data is processed. No user data is stored. All prices are synthetic GBM paths with realistic parameters.

6. **AI Assistance** — Development tools were used with full human oversight and mathematical verification of every formula.

## Cross-Domain Generalization

The mathematical machinery in RiskGraph is domain-agnostic. The same framework applies to:

| Domain | Nodes | Edges | Contagion |
|--------|-------|-------|-----------|
| Finance | Assets | Correlation | Capital flow herding |
| Epidemiology | Populations | Contact rate | Disease spread (SIR) |
| Infrastructure | Grid nodes | Physical links | Cascade failure |
| Social Networks | Users | Influence weight | Information contagion |
| Neuroscience | Brain regions | Functional connectivity | Neural cascade |

Spectral analysis, centrality computation, and contagion dynamics are identical across all domains. Only the interpretation of nodes, edges, and flow semantics changes.
